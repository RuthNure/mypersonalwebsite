About Baby Blues

Many of us feel the excitement and joy of having a newborn baby. Regardless of the sleepless nights and dirty diapers, we are ready for it all. However, it can also bring something we would never expect - depression. Postpartum depression is a challenging and often overwhelming experience that some new mothers can go through after giving birth. It goes beyond the typical mood swings and exhaustion that accompany motherhood, as it involves persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and emptiness that can linger for weeks or even months. This condition can manifest as extreme fatigue, irritability, and a sense of disconnection from the newborn. Many women with PPD experience guilt, self-blame, and a loss of interest in activities they once enjoyed. It can also affect physical health, leading to changes in appetite and sleep patterns. However, it's important to know that PPD is treatable, and seeking help from a healthcare provider, therapy, or support groups can make a significant difference in the journey to recovery, ultimately benefiting both the mother and the baby.

Postpartum depression affects 50-75% of new mothers.
